# AstroGrid Cosmology

## Core Field
Everything begins with computed state.

- bodies
- positions
- motions
- aspects
- cycles
- events
- signals

No invented numbers.

## Cultural Engines
Each practice reads the same sky.

Each keeps its own ledger.

Examples:
- Western
- Hellenistic
- Vedic
- Hermetic
- I Ching
- Kabbalistic
- Babylonian
- Maya
- Arabic
- Egyptian
- Taoist
- Tantric

## Lens Law
Belief systems are lenses.

They can be:
- isolated
- stacked
- intersected
- muted

The user must be able to decide.

## Lens Modes
- solo
- chorus
- intersection
- shadow

`solo`
: one tradition only

`chorus`
: many traditions side by side

`intersection`
: show overlap, agreement, fracture

`shadow`
: one primary lens with others dimmed

## Law
Do not blend traditions at the source.

Each engine should produce its own:
- reading
- omen
- prediction
- rationale
- confidence
- log

Keep them separate.

Do not force one tradition to speak in another tradition's frame.

## Log Structure
One sky.
Many readings.
Many ledgers.

For each engine, log:
- timestamp
- input sky state
- derived features used
- engine identity
- reading text
- prediction claims
- confidence
- horizon
- later outcome

These logs become memory.

## Seer Layer
Above the ledgers sits the final voice.

Working name:
- Seer

Other possible names:
- Witness
- Veil
- Oracle
- Chamber
- Black Glass

## Seer Law
The Seer does not replace the engines.

It reads across them.

It should:
- detect convergence
- detect contradiction
- weigh signal against history
- merge celestial readings with GRID knowledge
- speak tersely
- predict clearly

It may sound cryptic.

It may not be empty.

## Seer Inputs
- full computed sky state
- cultural engine outputs
- historical accuracy of each engine
- GRID macro and market state
- timing and event structure
- active lens selection

## Seer Outputs
- singular reading
- singular omen
- singular forecast
- confidence band
- key factors
- contradiction note when needed

## Logs Room
Logs are first-class.

Users should be able to inspect:
- what each engine said
- when it said it
- what happened after
- where the Seer was right
- where it drifted

Truth accrues.

## Intersections
Users may want distance.

Users may want convergence.

So AstroGrid should show:
- what one tradition says alone
- what many traditions say together
- where they agree
- where they diverge
- what disappears when a lens is removed

## Persona Layer
Above the engines sits an optional speaking mask.

Qwen can inhabit a chosen voice and answer questions.

Examples:
- Vedic reader
- Hermetic operator
- Hellenistic astrologer
- Taoist sage
- Babylonian observer
- Seer

## Persona Law
The persona is an interface.

Not the source.

Rules:
- persona must declare its lens
- persona must answer from the selected engine or stack
- persona must not silently mix forbidden lenses
- persona may cite intersections when the user allows it
- persona output should be logged

- the sky is computed
- the traditions are separated
- the outputs are logged
- the logs are judged
- GRID contributes worldly context

That is the edge.
