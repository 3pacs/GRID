# Historical Regime Reference

Use these historical episodes as reference points when analyzing current market conditions.

## 2007-2009: Global Financial Crisis

### Pre-Crisis Signals (2006-2007)
- Yield curve inverted in 2006 (2s10s went negative)
- HY spreads began widening in June 2007
- S&P 500 breadth deteriorated while index made new highs (July 2007)
- VIX started rising from historic lows
- VIIRS data (if it existed then) would have shown construction activity declining

### Crisis Phase (Sep 2008 - Mar 2009)
- VIX hit 89.53 (all-time high)
- HY spreads exploded to 2,000+ bps
- All correlations went to 1
- USD surged (dollar funding squeeze)
- Global trade collapsed 12% in 2009

### Key Lesson
Credit markets (HY spreads) signaled trouble 15 months before Lehman. Equity markets were in denial.

## 2010-2011: European Debt Crisis

### Build-Up
- Greece fiscal crisis exposed in late 2009
- BTP-Bund spread widened from 80 bps to 500+ bps
- ECB initially refused to act as lender of last resort
- Contagion from Greece → Ireland → Portugal → Spain → Italy

### Resolution
- ECB's "whatever it takes" (July 2012)
- BTP-Bund spread compressed immediately
- Risk-on rally followed

### Key Lesson
BTP-Bund spread was THE signal. Political will to backstop the currency union was the binary risk.

## 2015-2016: China Devaluation / Global Manufacturing Recession

### Sequence
- China devalued CNY in August 2015
- Chinese capital outflows accelerated ($1T in reserves burned)
- Global PMIs fell below 50
- HY spreads widened (energy sector stress)
- VIX spiked to 40+ briefly
- BUT: US services and labor market held up

### Key Lesson
Manufacturing recession ≠ full recession. Sector-specific credit stress (energy HY) didn't become systemic. Breadth outside energy was okay.

## 2018 Q4: Vol Shock / Fed Tightening Overshoot

### Sequence
- Fed raised rates 4 times in 2018 (too many)
- QT (Quantitative Tightening) was on autopilot
- VIX spiked in Feb 2018 (Volmageddon — XIV blew up)
- Q4 selloff: S&P down 20% from peak
- Credit spreads widened significantly
- Cu/Au ratio fell sharply

### Resolution
- Fed pivot in January 2019 (paused, then cut)
- Immediate V-shaped recovery

### Key Lesson
When the Fed is the problem, the Fed is also the solution. Watch for policy pivot signals.

## 2020: COVID Crash and Recovery

### Crash (Feb-Mar 2020)
- Fastest 30% drawdown in history
- VIX hit 82.69
- Credit markets froze (HY spreads to 1,100 bps)
- Dollar surged (global funding squeeze)
- Oil went NEGATIVE (April futures)
- All correlations = 1

### Recovery (Mar 2020 onward)
- Fed unlimited QE + emergency facilities (Mar 23)
- Fiscal stimulus ($2.2T CARES Act)
- V-shaped market recovery
- K-shaped economic recovery (rich recovered, poor didn't)
- Massive breadth divergence between growth and value

### Key Lesson
Speed of policy response was unprecedented. Traditional timing signals (wait for breadth confirmation) would have been too slow. The policy response WAS the signal.

## 2022: Inflation Shock / Rate Hiking Cycle

### Sequence
- CPI hit 9.1% (June 2022)
- Fed raised rates from 0% to 5.25% in 18 months
- Both stocks AND bonds fell (60/40 failure)
- Dollar surged to 20-year highs
- Duration assets (TLT) lost 40%+
- Growth stocks crushed (Nasdaq -33%)
- EM currencies under severe pressure

### Key Features
- Yield curve deeply inverted
- Real FFR went positive for first time in years
- Cu/Au ratio fell sharply
- VIX elevated but not extreme (unusual)
- Credit spreads widened but not crisis levels

### Key Lesson
Inflation changes everything. Traditional cross-asset correlations (stocks and bonds negatively correlated) broke down. Real rates were the dominant driver.

## 2023-2024: Soft Landing Debate

### Features
- Yield curve remained inverted for record duration
- Labor market stayed strong despite rate hikes
- Inflation gradually declining
- PMIs mixed (manufacturing weak, services strong)
- Credit spreads remarkably tight
- Equity breadth narrow then broadening
- Magnificent 7 concentration risk

### Key Lesson
Regime classification was genuinely uncertain for extended period. Mixed signals are themselves a regime (Transition). Patience was rewarded.

## Using Historical Analogies

When comparing current conditions to historical episodes:

1. **Match the configuration, not the narrative**: Focus on which features are in similar states
2. **Acknowledge differences**: No two episodes are identical
3. **Beware of anchoring**: The next crisis won't look like the last one
4. **Policy context matters**: Same market conditions with different central bank stances produce different outcomes
5. **Time horizon**: Some patterns play out in weeks, others in quarters
